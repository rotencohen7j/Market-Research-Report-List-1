<p><p>The Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) sensors market comprises two primary types: Attitude and Heading Reference System (AHRS) and Inertial Navigation System (INS). AHRS primarily focuses on providing orientation data by measuring angular velocity and magnetic fields, making it essential for applications requiring precise attitude information. In contrast, INS combines accelerometers and gyroscopes to calculate an object's position and velocity over time, enabling navigation without external references. Both systems play vital roles in aerospace, robotics, and automotive applications.</p></p>
